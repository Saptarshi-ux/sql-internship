# SQL Challenge Based Internship – Business Analytics Case Studies  

##  Description  
This repository contains my complete work from the **IVY SQL Challenge-Based Internship**, conducted from **October to November 2025**.  

The internship focused on solving **real-world business analytics problems using SQL**, covering domains such as:
- Sales Analysis  
- Customer Behavior  
- Inventory Management  
- Funnel Analysis  
- Cohort Analysis  
- Product Bundling Strategies  

Each challenge required translating business questions into efficient SQL logic, handling messy real-world data, and deriving meaningful insights for decision-making.

---

##  Tools & Technologies  
- **SQL (MySQL)**  
- **Window Functions** (ROW_NUMBER, NTILE, RANK, DENSE_RANK)  
- **Common Table Expressions (CTEs)** – `WITH` clauses  
- **Joins** (INNER, LEFT, FULL logic via UNION)  
- **Date & Time Analysis**  
- **Aggregation & Business Metrics**  

---

##  Key Business Problems Solved  

### 🔹 Customer & Sales Analytics  
- Identified high-value and inactive customers based on session behavior  
- Built **purchase funnels** from web events  
- Performed **cohort analysis** to study repeat purchase behavior  

---

### 🔹 Product & Revenue Analysis  
- Identified top revenue-generating products (**Hero Products**)  
- Conducted **market basket analysis** (product combinations)  
- Measured **basket value uplift** for bundled products  

---

### 🔹 Inventory vs Demand Alignment  
- Compared **monthly inventory movements vs monthly sales**  
- Classified products as:  
  - Within Demand  
  -  Inventory Issue  
- Highlighted stock planning gaps at **store–product–month level**  

---

### 🔹 Advanced SQL Concepts  
- Performed time-based analysis using `MAX(event_datetime)` instead of system date  
- Handled **anonymous users (NULL customer_id)** with logical filtering  
- Applied **normalization and ranking techniques** to identify popular and profitable product combinations  

---

## Key Learnings  
- Strengthened ability to convert **business problems into SQL queries**  
- Gained experience in **real-world messy datasets**  
- Improved skills in **data transformation, joins, and window functions**  
- Developed a balance between **technical accuracy and business interpretation**
